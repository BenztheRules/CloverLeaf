Make a Lily
1.

Take medium sized or larger square sheet, flip white side up. Fold from corner to corner.

2.

Crease along diagonal making sure it's lined up. Turn 90 degrees, and repeat.

3.

Fold triangle at crease into 1/4 sized triangle

4.

Crease the fold.

5.

Open flap from step 4. Press the corner down to meet the other corner.

6.

Crease the fold, resulting in a square section. There should be an outward facing crease diagonally from corner to corner.

7.

Flip over so the square is now the base.

8.

Repeat step 5 and 6.

9.

Open triangle up and fold in half diagonally on the crease. The square should fold naturally with the crease and the color should be on the inside.

10.

Repeat steps 5 and 6 once more on this side.

11.

There should now be four flaps, two on top and two on bottom. Take on of those flapsand stand it upright.

12.

In a similar fashion to steps 5 and 6, open the flap and press down. This should result in a triangle from the top to the end of the color.

13.

Grab the left side flap from the piece you just made. Starting at the point nearest you, fold the outside flap to line up with the center line.

14.

Repeat step 13, making sure the two folds line up with the middle as well as possible.

15.

Open those two flaps up, and create a crease from the corners of the creases you just made. this will serve as a guide in the next step.

16.

This is one of the more difficult techniques to get right at first so keep trying it until it looks right. Lift the center crease at the base of the colored triangle. Using the crease vertex you just drew with your finger, flip the bottom flap up. Be sure the two side flaps come with evenly so that when you push it down, they line up evenly.

17.

Fold top of flap down over the finger drawn crease.

18.

Repeat steps 12 through 17 on the 3 remaining flaps.

19.

After all sides are complete, it should look like this. Now lay it down flat on the table, with the solid end on the bottom and the flat side facing up.

20.

Fold from the bottom point matching the outside edge with the inside middle crease.

21.

Repeat step 20 for the opposite flap. Repeate for all four sides.

22.

Hold with the creased flaps in your hand and grab the top portion of that side.

23.

Crease the flap over towards the bottom. Repeate for all four sides.

24.

Open flower up and grab a pedal of the Lily. Roll over finger or pencil to give it a realistic droop.
